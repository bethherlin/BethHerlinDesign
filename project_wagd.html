Wagd

Product Development and Design

41% of dogs in the US suffer from anxiety/fear related issues, affecting a total of 26 million homes. We want to ENABLE parents to engage with their dog remotely, and EMPOWER them to mitigate that anxiety while away from home.

Competitive Landscape

To effectively address situational anxiety in dogs, we identified three important areas that our product must inhabit to be adaptive. No products in the anxiety reduction space currently address all three areas.

Phase 3

Conceptualization

Using the 3-6-5 brainstorming method, my team and I created a variety of concepts to address our opportunity. We then used affinity diagramming to organize our ideas into coherent concept designs.

Pugh Analysis

This method allowed us to compare our product concepts to our most direct competitors (which was actually a combination of existing products). We scored our 9 concepts and 2 competitors against a datum across criteria for the parent, dog, and our company stakeholder.

Concept Selection

After consolidating information from both user research and the Pugh Analysis, the cuddle toy and smart bed were revealed to be the most promising concepts.

Usability Testing

A functional prototype for the cuddle toy concept was developed and testing, revealing that dogs view the form more as a play toy than a mechanism for rest. This led us to shift our focus to the smart bed concept. To test the mechanism that would go inside of the smart bed, we constructed a cloth-covered frame, housing a wireless speaker. Pheromone was sprayed on the inside of the frame. Testing suggested the concept was viable.

Concept Sketching and Refinement

To further explore how the bed would incorporate the functionalities of a pheromone dispenser and heartbeat/music player, I designed the general specifications for the product.

Phase 4

Final Product

Interface Flow

I developed a heirarchy of information based on research insights to design a mobile application that both conveys monitor data to the parent, as well as provides remote control over the anxiety reducer at home

Interface Development & Testing

I progressed the interface through three different iterations alongside usability testing in the form of cognitive walkthroughs and informal interviews.

Smart Bed Mechanism

The ABS housing, located in the rim of the bed, contains a mechanism to spray pheromone into a filter pad, where a fan intakes the pheromone safely through the mesh cover, towards the interior of the bed. The box also contains a speaker to display the hearbeat and music.

Collar Monitor

The device on the collar contains a tri-axis accelerometer (to measure activity level), a pulsometer (to measure heart rate), microphone (to measure vocalization), and RFID tag (to measure proximity to the bed).

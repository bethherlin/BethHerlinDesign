Clinical Decision Support Repository

User research, literature research, UX / UI design

Administrators, clinicians, and technologists of health systems have no standardized resource for reputable clinical decision support (CDS), and no global metric to evaluate the effectiveness, trustworthiness, and risk of implementing a given CDS artifact. AHRQ contracted MITRE, a non-profit government R&D company, to create a central repository to help clinicians easily find, evaluate, and implement reputable CDS artifacts at their institutions.

The Client

The MITRE Corporation in collaboration with AHRQ

The Project Scope

In collaboration with an internal health consultant, I managed the project and worked directly with leadership on the MITRE team to understand the problem set and gather insights, conduct expert interviews with stakeholders and consultants throughout and outside of AHRQ, iteratively design mockups and prototypes for review with the AHRQ work group, and develop front end code for certain aspects of the interface.

Project Outcome

Work evolving the design of the repository is still ongoing, and CDS content will be filled in over time. The repository is live at https://cds.ahrq.gov/cdsconnect
